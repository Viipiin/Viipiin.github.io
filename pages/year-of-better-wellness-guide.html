Part 01

Mindset
before method.

Before food, before workouts — fix the way you think about change. Most people fail not because the plan is wrong, but because they're trying to be a different person in 7 days.

Start ridiculously small. A 10-minute walk beats a planned 1-hour gym session you skip. Don't aim for perfect — aim for "never two bad days in a row." One bad meal is a meal. Two bad weeks is a lifestyle.

Replace, don't remove. Swap soda for sparkling water with lemon. Swap the cigarette for a five-minute walk. Removing leaves a void; replacing fills it.

The "21 days to form a habit" thing is a myth. Real research says 60 to 90 days. Plan for the long road.

Daily non-negotiables

These five tiny commitments — kept every day — are worth more than any diet or training program. Stack them first.

01

Water First

500 ml within 30 minutes of waking. Before tea, before coffee, before anything.

02

Move 20 Min

Walk, stretch, dance, train — anything. Just move the body daily.

03

One Protein Meal

At least one meal a day built around real protein. Eggs, paneer, fish, lentils.

04

Seven Hours Sleep

Non-negotiable. Cap caffeine at 2 PM. Phone out of bed.

05

Screen Curfew

No screens 30 minutes before bed. The body needs the signal that the day is over.

Part 03

Build the
plate.

Forget calorie counting for a moment. If every meal you eat follows this one rule, you'll lose weight and feel better — guaranteed.

The simple plate rule

  • ½ plate — vegetables (sabzi, salad, raw or cooked, any color)
  • ¼ plate — lean protein (chicken, fish, eggs, paneer, tofu, dal, rajma, chickpeas)
  • ¼ plate — complex carbs (brown rice, whole-wheat roti, dalia, millet roti, oats, sweet potato)
  • 1 thumb — healthy fats (ghee, mustard oil, groundnut oil, nuts, seeds)

palak & greens cauliflower cabbage lauki, tinda, parwal tomatoes eggs local fish chicken breast dahi / hung curd paneer tofu dals & rajma oats & dalia millets sweet potato nuts, seeds, makhana ghee, mustard oil

Eat sometimes

whole-grain bread full-fat dairy dark chocolate 70%+ red meat white rice fruit juices

Eat rarely

refined sugar white flour deep-fried foods sugary drinks packaged snacks alcohol processed meats

Part 06

Smoothies, lassis
& sharbats.

One glass, one purpose — built with ingredients you'll actually find at your local sabziwala and kirana store. Each recipe is sized for one person, with the final glass volume noted. Curd replaces yogurt. Banana and mango replace berries. Sattu and peanuts deliver protein at a fraction of the cost of whey.

The universal method

  1. Liquid first — water, milk, buttermilk, or coconut water goes in first. Protects the blade.
  2. Soft ingredients next — banana, dahi (curd), soaked oats, nut butter, soaked sattu.
  3. Greens & hard fruit — palak, lauki, carrot, apple.
  4. Frozen items & ice last — they sit on top and get pulled down.
  5. Blend 45–60 seconds until smooth. Drink within 15 minutes for best taste.

Budget tip: hung curd = thick yogurt at a third the price. Just tie regular dahi in a clean cotton cloth, hang for 1 hour, water drains out. Use it like Greek yogurt anywhere.

Indian-kitchen tips

Substitutions that work everywhere: Greek yogurt → hung curd · almond milk → regular milk or coconut milk · honey → jaggery (gur) · berries → pomegranate, banana, papaya, mango · maple syrup → jaggery syrup · quinoa → dalia (broken wheat) or millet.

Pick four favorites — one weight-loss, one protein, one gut, one glow. Buy ingredients for those. Variety is great in theory but ruinous to grocery bills.

One smoothie a day, not three. Even healthy smoothies are calorie-dense. Replace one meal — don't add three drinks to your day.

Part 08

Train like a
beginner.

Three levers: walking (daily), strength training (3× per week — highest ROI for fat loss and body shape), and cardio (2× per week for heart health). That's it. Ignore everything else for now.

Weeks 1–2 · Foundation (no equipment)

7,000 steps daily. Three 20-minute sessions a week. You can do all of this on a yoga mat at home.

  • 10 squats
  • 10 knee push-ups
  • 10 glute bridges
  • 20-second plank
  • 20 jumping jacks

Rest 60 seconds. Three rounds. Bonus: add 5 rounds of surya namaskar in the morning for full-body warm-up + flexibility.

Weeks 3–4 · Build

8,000 steps daily. Three strength sessions + two brisk 30-minute walks. Bump the reps:

  • 15 squats
  • 12 push-ups (full or incline against a wall)
  • 15 glute bridges
  • 30-second plank
  • 12 lunges per leg
  • 15 rows using a backpack filled with books or water bottles

Three rounds. Rest 45 seconds.

Weeks 5–8 · Real training

10,000 steps daily. Four sessions a week, alternating A · B · C.

Budget home setup (under ₹3,000)

  • Yoga mat — ₹400–600
  • Resistance band set — ₹300–500
  • One pair of 5kg adjustable dumbbells or a kettlebell — ₹1,500–2,500
  • Skipping rope — ₹150

You can train hard for years on just this. No gym membership needed.

The four laws of fat loss training

  1. Lift before cardio if doing both in one session.
  2. Progressive overload. Add one rep or a tiny bit of weight every week.
  3. Walk 10–15 minutes after meals. Surprisingly powerful for blood sugar — and very Indian-friendly (we're a walking culture).
  4. Don't "earn" food with workouts. Diet drives weight loss; training drives shape and strength.

Days 1–3 · Prepare

  • Pick a quit date. Tell three people.
  • Throw away all cigarettes, lighters, ashtrays. Wash clothes that smell.
  • Identify your triggers — after meals, with tea, when stressed, when drinking.
  • Decide on a replacement for each trigger:
    • After meals → 5-minute walk + chewing gum
    • With tea → switch to green tea, hold the cup differently
    • Stress → 4-7-8 breathing (inhale 4 sec, hold 7, exhale 8)
    • Drinking → quit alcohol simultaneously for the first 30 days

Days 4–14 · The hard part

Cravings peak around day 3–5 and each one lasts about 3–5 minutes. Set a 5-minute timer when one hits. Wait it out.

  • Use nicotine replacement (patches, gum, lozenges) if needed — ask a doctor.
  • Drink lots of water. Suck on cinnamon sticks or chew cloves.
  • Exercise hard. Sweating reduces cravings.
  • No alcohol. It kills willpower.

Days 15–30 · Lock it in

  • Reward milestones with non-food rewards: a new shirt, a book, a movie.
  • Save the cigarette money in a visible jar. Watch it grow.
  • Join a community — Reddit r/stopsmoking, QuitNow app.

You are now a non-smoker. Not "trying to quit." Practice saying it: "I don't smoke."

If you slip: one cigarette is a mistake. Going back to a pack is a choice. Get right back on day one.

Part 11

Cut down on
alcohol.

Alcohol = empty calories + worse sleep + worse decisions (food, smoking) + slower recovery. Two paths work; choose the one you can stick to.

Path A · Moderation

  • Max 2–3 drinks, 1–2 days a week.
  • Never on consecutive days.
  • One glass of water between every drink.
  • Eat protein before drinking.
  • Stop ordering by default. Make every drink a deliberate choice.

Path B · 30/60/90 days sober

  • Easier than moderation for many people.
  • After 30 days: better sleep, clearer skin.
  • Easier weight loss, more money saved.
  • More mornings worth having.
  • You'll be surprised what you don't miss.

Replacement drinks for social situations

sparkling water + lime + bitters soda + cranberry + lime non-alcoholic beer mocktails (low sugar) kombucha

If you cannot stop after starting, or drink daily and feel dependent, that's not a willpower issue — it's a medical one. Talk to a doctor. There's no shame in it.

Part 12

For the
lazy cook.

Be honest: laziness around food is the number one reason healthy eating fails. The fix isn't motivation. It's removing decisions and effort.

The five rules of lazy healthy eating

01

Decide once

Pick 3 breakfasts, 3 lunches, 3 dinners. Rotate. Stop choosing.

02

Cook in bulk

One hour Sunday = four days of meals.

03

Pre-assemble parts

Boiled eggs, cooked dal, chopped veg, cooked rice, hummus, yogurt — always ready.

04

Zero junk at home

If it's not there, you can't eat it at 11 PM.

05

Order smart

Subway sub, grilled chicken thali, sushi, salad bowl. Healthy takeout exists.

5-minute meals · no cooking skill required

  • Greek yogurt + chopped fruit + nuts + honey
  • 2 boiled eggs + whole-grain toast + tomato + pepper
  • Hummus + carrots + cucumber + 1 pita
  • Paneer + chopped tomato + olive oil + salt + pepper + 1 roti
  • Pre-cooked dal (microwave) + pre-cooked rice + frozen veg sautéed 5 min

The 60-minute Sunday prep

  1. Boil 12 eggs → fridge.
  2. Cook 2 cups brown rice or quinoa → fridge.
  3. Cook a big pot of dal → fridge.
  4. Chop a tub of salad veggies → fridge.
  5. Roast a tray of veggies (broccoli, sweet potato, peppers, olive oil, salt) at 200°C for 25 min → fridge.
  6. Grill 500 g chicken or marinate 500 g paneer/tofu → fridge.

You now have four days of mix-and-match healthy meals — assembled, not cooked.

Part 13

Sleep, stress
and recovery.

You can't out-train or out-diet bad sleep. If only one thing on this list gets your attention, make it this one.

Sleep checklist

  • Same bedtime, same wake time — even on weekends.
  • Cool, dark, quiet room. Blackout curtains help a lot.
  • No screens 30 minutes before bed. Read a physical book.
  • No caffeine after 2 PM.
  • No alcohol within 3 hours of bed.
  • Last meal 2–3 hours before bed.
  • Aim for 7–9 hours.

Stress decompression

Cortisol is the fat-storage, craving-driving, sleep-wrecking hormone. Daily decompression keeps it in check.

  • 10-minute walk outdoors, phone away
  • 5 minutes of box breathing (4-4-4-4)
  • Three-line journal: what went well, what I'm grateful for, tomorrow's #1 priority
  • One hobby with nothing to do with productivity

Recovery

  • One full rest day per week
  • Stretch 5–10 minutes daily
  • Foam rolling or self-massage if accessible
  • Sauna or hot showers — both help muscle recovery
