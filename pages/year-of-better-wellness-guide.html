Part 01

Mindset
before method.

Before food, before workouts — fix the way you think about change. Most people fail not because the plan is wrong, but because they're trying to be a different person in 7 days.

Start ridiculously small. A 10-minute walk beats a planned 1-hour gym session you skip. Don't aim for perfect — aim for "never two bad days in a row." One bad meal is a meal. Two bad weeks is a lifestyle.

Replace, don't remove. Swap soda for sparkling water with lemon. Swap the cigarette for a five-minute walk. Removing leaves a void; replacing fills it.

"The 21 days to form a habit thing is a myth. Real research says 60 to 90 days. Plan for the long road."

Daily non-negotiables

These five tiny commitments — kept every day — are worth more than any diet or training program. Stack them first.

01

Water First

500 ml within 30 minutes of waking. Before tea, before coffee, before anything.

02

Move 20 Min

Walk, stretch, dance, train — anything. Just move the body daily.

03

One Protein Meal

At least one meal a day built around real protein. Eggs, paneer, fish, lentils.

04

Seven Hours Sleep

Non-negotiable. Cap caffeine at 2 PM. Phone out of bed.

05

Screen Curfew

No screens 30 minutes before bed. The body needs the signal that the day is over.

The habit psychology

Every habit has three parts: cue → routine → reward. To build a good habit, attach it to an existing one (habit stacking). To break a bad one, disrupt the cue. The cigarette after chai? Change the chai ritual first.

Identity is the deepest lever. Don't say "I'm trying to quit smoking." Say "I'm not a smoker." Don't say "I'm trying to eat healthy." Say "I'm someone who eats clean." Every action is a vote for your identity.

Gut health basics

Your gut microbiome influences weight, mood, immunity, and energy. Feed it well with: fibre-rich foods (dal, oats, vegetables), fermented foods (dahi, chaas, idli, dosa), and diversity — aim for 30 different plant foods per week. Reduce ultra-processed food, excess sugar, and unnecessary antibiotics. A healthy gut is the foundation everything else rests on.

Realistic cheat meal strategy

One cheat meal per week — not a cheat day. A single indulgent meal won't derail progress. A full day of eating whatever you want can easily wipe out a week's deficit. The rules: enjoy it fully, no guilt → get back on plan with the very next meal. Don't "earn" cheat meals with extra workouts — food is not a reward or punishment.

Part 06

Smoothies, lassis
& sharbats.

One glass, one purpose — built with ingredients from your local sabziwala and kirana. Each recipe serves one. Sattu and peanuts deliver protein at a fraction of whey's cost.

The universal method

  1. Liquid first — water, milk, buttermilk, or coconut water. Protects the blade.
  2. Soft ingredients next — banana, dahi, soaked oats, nut butter, soaked sattu.
  3. Greens & hard fruit — palak, lauki, carrot, apple.
  4. Frozen items & ice last — they get pulled down.
  5. Blend 45–60 seconds until smooth. Drink within 15 minutes.

Budget tip: hung curd = thick yogurt at a third the price. Just tie regular dahi in a clean cotton cloth, hang for 1 hour, water drains out.

Part 08

Train like a
beginner.

Three levers: walking (daily), strength training (3× per week — highest ROI for fat loss and body shape), and cardio (2× per week for heart health). That's it.

8-Week Beginner Roadmap

Weeks 1–2
Foundation — no equipment

7,000 steps daily. Three 20-min sessions/week on a yoga mat.

10 squats · 10 knee push-ups · 10 glute bridges · 20-sec plank · 20 jumping jacks. Rest 60 sec. 3 rounds. Bonus: 5 rounds of surya namaskar in the morning.

Weeks 3–4
Build — bump the reps

8,000 steps daily. Three strength sessions + two 30-min brisk walks.

15 squats · 12 push-ups (full or incline) · 15 glute bridges · 30-sec plank · 12 lunges/leg · 15 rows (backpack filled with books). 3 rounds. Rest 45 sec.

Weeks 5–8
Real training — four sessions per week

10,000 steps daily. Alternate Day A · B · C + one rest day.

Month 3+
Add load — resistance bands or dumbbells

Progressive overload: add 1 rep or slightly more weight every week. Walk 10–15 min after meals for blood sugar control.

The four laws of fat-loss training

  1. Lift before cardio if doing both in one session.
  2. Progressive overload. Add one rep or a tiny bit of weight every week — this is the entire secret to getting stronger.
  3. Walk 10–15 minutes after meals. Surprisingly powerful for blood sugar — and very Indian-friendly.
  4. Don't "earn" food with workouts. Diet drives weight loss; training drives shape and strength. They're not the same thing.

Days 1–3 · Prepare

  • Pick a quit date. Tell three people.
  • Throw away all cigarettes, lighters, ashtrays. Wash clothes that smell.
  • Identify your triggers — after meals, with tea, when stressed, when drinking.
  • Decide on a replacement for each trigger: after meals → 5-min walk + chewing gum · with chai → switch to green tea, hold the cup differently · stress → 4-7-8 breathing · drinking → quit alcohol for the first 30 days

Days 4–14 · The hard part

Cravings peak around day 3–5 and each one lasts about 3–5 minutes. Set a 5-minute timer when one hits. Wait it out.

  • Use nicotine replacement (patches, gum, lozenges) if needed — ask a doctor.
  • Drink lots of water. Suck on cinnamon sticks or chew cloves.
  • Exercise hard. Sweating reduces cravings.
  • No alcohol. It kills willpower.

Days 15–30 · Lock it in

  • Reward milestones with non-food rewards: a new shirt, a book, a movie.
  • Save the cigarette money in a visible jar. Watch it grow.
  • Join a community — Reddit r/stopsmoking, QuitNow app.
You are now a non-smoker. Not "trying to quit." Practice saying it: "I don't smoke."

If you slip: one cigarette is a mistake. Going back to a pack is a choice. Get right back on day one. Progress is not lost.

Part 11

Cut down on
alcohol.

Alcohol = empty calories + worse sleep + worse decisions (food, smoking) + slower recovery. Two paths work; choose the one you can stick to.

Path A · Moderation

  • Max 2–3 drinks, 1–2 days a week.
  • Never on consecutive days.
  • One glass of water between every drink.
  • Eat protein before drinking.
  • Stop ordering by default. Make every drink a deliberate choice.

Path B · 30/60/90 days sober

  • Easier than moderation for many people.
  • After 30 days: better sleep, clearer skin.
  • Easier weight loss, more money saved.
  • More mornings worth having.
  • You'll be surprised what you don't miss.

Replacement drinks for social situations

sparkling water + lime + bitters soda + cranberry + lime non-alcoholic beer mocktails (low sugar) kombucha nimbu soda + black salt

If you cannot stop after starting, or drink daily and feel dependent, that's not a willpower issue — it's a medical one. Talk to a doctor. There's no shame in it.

Part 15 · New

Mental
wellness.

The body and mind are one system. You can't sustainably transform the physical without addressing the mental. This is where most health journeys quietly fail.

Core pillars of mental wellness

🧠

Stress Management

Chronic stress is a physical disease. Cortisol disrupts sleep, inflames the body, and drives fat storage — especially belly fat. Daily decompression is not optional.

📵

Dopamine Detox

Constant novelty (social media, reels, notifications) destroys focus and makes real-life rewards feel boring. Schedule 1 day/week of reduced screen time. The boredom passes. Clarity doesn't.

🧘

Mindfulness

10 min/day of meditation changes brain structure in 8 weeks — proven by MRI. Start with Headspace or Calm. Or simply sit, breathe, and notice thoughts without chasing them.

🍔

Emotional Eating

Boredom, loneliness, and stress are the real triggers. Before eating outside a mealtime, ask: am I physically hungry? If not, what am I actually feeling? The answer is usually the real need.

📱

Screen-Time Management

Set a phone screen-time limit. Move social apps off the home screen. Keep the first and last 30 min of your day phone-free. Reclaim 1–2 hours/day of your actual life.

🌱

Digital Detox Protocol

One screen-free morning per week. One full digital-detox day per month. No notifications during meals. No phone in the bedroom. Watch how much mental space opens up.

Journaling prompts to start with

Three minutes in the morning or before bed. You don't need to write perfectly — just write honestly.

What would make today a great day, even if just one small thing?
What am I avoiding that, if I faced it, would change everything?
What's one way I treated my body well today? And one way I didn't?
Who do I want to be in 12 months? What would that person do today?
What am I most grateful for right now — something I usually take for granted?
What story am I telling myself about why I can't change? Is it actually true?

Longevity habits — the ones science agrees on

These are the behaviours most consistently linked to living longer, healthier, with more cognitive function:

  • 7–9 hours of quality sleep, every night
  • Strong social connections (the #1 longevity predictor)
  • Regular movement — any kind, every day
  • Sense of purpose — something you wake up for
  • Low chronic stress + daily decompression
  • Mostly whole foods, minimal ultra-processed
  • Not smoking. Minimal alcohol.
  • Managing weight — specifically visceral fat around the abdomen

Reverse fatty liver,
shrink the visceral fat.

Fatty liver and stubborn belly fat are two faces of the same problem — too much fat stored where it shouldn't be. The fix isn't a crash diet. It's a sustained reset that plays out a little differently for men and women.

Why men and women get here differently

Fatty liver (NAFLD) and visceral fat share root causes — excess sugar, low movement, poor sleep — but the risk factors and warning lines differ by sex. Know yours.

For Men

  • Visceral fat collects first — even a "skinny fat" man can have a fatty liver.
  • Alcohol is the single biggest accelerant; daily "social" drinking adds up faster than it feels like it does.
  • Waist over 90 cm (35.5") is a stronger early warning than BMI.
  • Responds fast to resistance training — added muscle directly improves how the body clears blood sugar.

For Women

  • Estrogen is protective before menopause — risk rises sharply after, even without weight gain.
  • PCOS roughly doubles fatty-liver risk, independent of body weight.
  • Waist over 80 cm (31.5") is the equivalent warning line.
  • Crash diets and yo-yo weight cycling worsen liver fat — slow, steady loss outperforms extreme cuts.

The science, in one paragraph

Fatty liver is reversible for most people. Losing 7% of body weight meaningfully reduces liver fat; losing 10% can reverse early fibrosis in many cases. Diet drives the fat loss itself — but exercise, even without weight loss, independently shrinks liver fat by improving how liver and muscle handle sugar and fat. Combined, the two work faster than either alone.

Daily routine to shrink liver & visceral fat

On waking
Water, light, movement

500 ml water, then 10–15 min of walking in daylight before breakfast — improves insulin sensitivity for the rest of the day. Black coffee, if you take it, fits here too.

Overnight window
A gentle 12–14 hr fast

Dinner by 8 PM, first meal after 8–10 AM. Not extreme fasting — just a consistent overnight gap that gives the liver uninterrupted time to clear fat.

Daytime, 5×/week
150–200+ min of cardio

Brisk walking, cycling, or swimming. This is the single most evidence-backed lever for liver fat — it works even before the scale moves.

3×/week
Strength training

Squats, rows, push-ups, lunges — bodyweight or light weights. More muscle means better blood-sugar clearance, which directly reduces liver fat storage.

After every meal
A 10–15 min walk

Blunts the blood-sugar spike that drives fat storage in the liver. The cheapest, easiest habit on this whole list.

Evening
Early dinner, no alcohol, real sleep

Light dinner finished 3 hrs before bed, zero alcohol, screens off 30 min before sleep, 7–9 hrs of rest. Poor sleep and chronic stress both independently drive visceral fat.
